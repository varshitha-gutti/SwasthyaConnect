Pillar 02 · Hygiene

The small routines that prevent big problems.

Most preventable illness traces back to a handful of hygiene basics. None of them are complicated — they just need to become automatic.

Handwashing

The single highest-impact habit on this page.

01

Wet, soap, scrub — 20 seconds

Cover palms, backs of hands, between fingers, and under nails.

02

Before eating or cooking

And after using the toilet, handling waste, or touching shared surfaces.

03

After coughing or sneezing

Or after touching your face, phone, or money.

04

No soap? Use sanitiser

Look for at least 60% alcohol content, and rub until fully dry.

Food Hygiene

Clean, separate, cook, chill.

Wash produce before eating, keep raw meat separate from other food, cook to safe temperatures, and refrigerate leftovers promptly. When unsure whether water is safe, boil it before drinking.

Home & Surroundings

A clean home is a quieter health risk.

Regularly clean frequently-touched surfaces (door handles, switches, phone screens), ensure proper waste disposal, and keep stagnant water away from your home to reduce mosquito breeding.

Menstrual Hygiene

Comfort and safety, without stigma.

✓

Change every 4–6 hours

Whichever product you use — pad, tampon, or cup — change regularly to avoid infection.

✓

Wash hands before and after

This applies whether changing, inserting, or removing a menstrual product.

✓

Dispose of products properly

Wrap before disposal; never flush pads or tampons.

✓

Track unusual symptoms

Unusual pain, odour, or irregular cycles are worth discussing with a doctor.

"Hygiene isn't about being clean once — it's about a routine you don't have to think about anymore."

Pillar 01 · Physical Health

Four habits that hold everything else up.

Sleep, nutrition, movement, and stress management aren't separate categories — they reinforce each other. Start with whichever feels most fixable this week.

Sleep

Protect your 7–9 hours.

01

Keep a consistent schedule

Same sleep and wake time daily, even on weekends, stabilises your body clock.

02

Dim screens before bed

Reduce blue-light exposure an hour before sleeping; switch to night mode if you must use a device.

03

Watch caffeine timing

Avoid caffeine after early afternoon — it can linger in your system for 6+ hours.

04

Cool, dark, quiet room

A slightly cool room and minimal light/noise improve sleep depth, not just duration.

Nutrition

Build a balanced plate, not a perfect diet.

Rather than chasing strict diet rules, aim for a simple plate ratio at most meals: half vegetables and fruit, a quarter whole grains, a quarter protein. Add healthy fats in moderation and limit ultra-processed snacks and sugary drinks.

+

Hydration

8–10 glasses of water daily; more in heat, illness, or after exercise.

+

Reduce added sugar

Check labels — sugar hides in sauces, drinks, and packaged snacks.

Stress & Mental Wellbeing

Small resets, used often, beat big resets used rarely.

Breathe in for 4 seconds, out for 6 seconds, for about 2 minutes. The longer exhale activates your body's calming response.

Simply labelling an emotion ("I'm feeling overwhelmed") can reduce its intensity — it's a well-documented effect in psychology.

If low mood, anxiety, or stress persists for more than two weeks and affects daily life, it's worth speaking with a doctor, counsellor, or trusted person.
